For Eric Francksen A Fifteen-Year
Partnership

Partnership is the word I keep coming back to when I think about you. Not friendship alone, not collaboration alone. Partnership. The whole shape of it. This is a small thing I made to say so plainly.

Prepared by Hand · Los Angeles · May 2026 — Gordon
Eric,

I went back through fifteen years of our emails this week. Not for any reason except that I wanted to. What I found was a kind of road map: you helping me build the IGDA, the two of us at a folding table for GDC, our team at GGP, Bec rolling dice on a Saturday, our Friday Tree meeting on my calendar tomorrow.

The word that kept rising to the surface was partnership. Not friendship by itself. Not collaboration by itself. Partnership, which is the harder and rarer thing. You and I have built actual things together. We have shown up for each other in the boring middle parts where most partnerships quietly die. We have learned each other's working rhythms, our families, our humor, our limits. After fifteen years I know what you will say before you say it, and I know I can hand you any half-formed idea and trust you to make it real.

I am not always good at slowing down to name what is right in front of me. You know this. So I made you this, because I wanted to put the receipts in one place and tell you what I see when I look at all of it together: a partnership that has lasted, evolved, and gotten better every single year.

This is for you.

With love, Gordon
I.

On the Word Itself

Part·ner·ship
A thing built by two people, with two pairs of hands, over enough years that no one can quite tell anymore whose idea was whose.
What I see when I read fifteen years of our emails in one sitting: I cannot point to a single major thing I have built in this industry without your fingerprints somewhere on it. The IGDA reboot. The GGP we have today. The Zero Proof Game Night. The IGNITE Impact Challenge. Even the Dragon House table. Our things, not mine. That is what the word means.

2011
Where It Begins

IGDA, Business Development Department

Eric@igda.org · Gordon@igda.org · MBA Candidate 2012

Eric, before you were my friend, you were on my team. By the summer of 2011 you were in IGDA's Business Development department, working under me, signing your emails "Eric Francksen, MBA Candidate 2012, Phone: 608-322-6841."

The phone number hasn't changed in fifteen years. Neither has the work ethic. The earliest email I have from you is July 14, 2011, on a weekly staff call. By August 2 you were writing me, while I was traveling in China, with a research report you'd assembled on Game Developer Mag advertisers because I'd asked for it on the phone. By August you were also handling Salesforce documentation, newsletter ad rates, the Global Game Jam sponsorship, the Jira migration. You were quietly making the trains run.

And then, on July 26, 2011, our first non-work email exchange. You sent me your debit card to use, and I replied: "So 5pm at Gym Bar? Your game is at 7p." That is the day work-friend became friend-friend, in writing.

2012
The Naming

Friends With Dodgeball Benefits

Throwmetheus · WeHo Dodgeball · "I'll be there, shoes and all"

In April 2012, you introduced me to George Allen at Curious Pictures. "Gordon is currently the CEO of the IGDA..." By May, we were on the same WeHo Dodgeball team. The roster was called Throwmetheus. Scott Evans captained it and the threads were beautifully chaotic.

On May 23, 2012, I sent the email that I think is the founding document of our chosen-family chapter. To Jake and Miles at Dodgeball4Ever: "Gordon Bellamy and Eric, are FwD (Friends with Dodgeball Benefits)." I named it. And then, in July 2024, twelve years later, you forwarded that same email back to me with no commentary. You kept the joke. You returned it. That is the whole friendship in one gesture.

By July I had you over for "Birthdaylimpics" at the house in North Hills. By December I brought you as my +1 to the Hideo Kojima VGA After-Party. Once you were inside my world, you stayed.

Skyship Studios · Gloom Digital · Blessed Sacrament Church, La Crosse

You were running Skyship Studios, building the digital Gloom, pulling me into the inner ring on Kickstarter rewards, audio, and art direction. By May, you invited me to your son's baptism. The email subject was three words long: "Francksen Fun Time!"

Wisconsin

Stoughton · Human Head Studios · GGP at PAX South

By 2017 I was on Paul Goemans' Viking Brew Pub mug club list. Stoughton, Wisconsin. The "Rewired Squirrel is back on tap" emails started showing up in my inbox the same way they showed up in yours. I was no longer your friend who visited Wisconsin. I was on the list.

In July 2018 you came to me for help: UI designers for Human Head Studios. The way I knew to ask "local in Madison or remote?" without explaining anything is its own kind of intimacy. We had a shared vocabulary by then.

And in December 2019, the inflection that would matter most. To Doc at Niantic, on a GGP PAX South thread: "I'm adding Eric Francksen, who is helping GGP produce this event." That sentence is the moment the partnership crossed into the work, in writing. The title Executive Producer comes later, but the partnership starts here.

V.

Six Things This Partnership Has Given Me

No. 1

That You Stayed.

People drift out of each other's lives all the time. We started as colleagues at IGDA in 2011, you could have moved on a dozen times since then, and instead you became closer. That is a choice. I see it.

No. 2

Your Phone Number.

608-322-6841. Same since at least July 2011. There is something quietly profound about a person who keeps their phone number for a decade and a half. It is a small contract you keep with everyone who ever needed to find you.

No. 3

The Way We Build.

I have ideas. You have follow-through. The 2nd Annual Zero Proof Game Night. The IGNITE Impact Challenge website launching on time. The DMs paid by Friday. The GGP Weekly Connect that simply happens every week. This is what partnership looks like in practice: nobody asks whose job it was, because the answer is always both of us, in our own ways, at the same time.

No. 4

You Brought Me Wisconsin.

Mug 88 at the Viking Brew Pub. Björn Hug IPA emails. James, Henry, and Oscar. Your distant cousin Kay. Wilhelm Francksen of the Civil War. You did not have to give me your home. You gave it to me anyway.

No. 5

The Dragon House Table.

You did not just invite me to play. You built the table, found the players, scheduled the sessions, and let Darby live. To make a chosen-family ritual in 2026, on Zoom, with Wizards of the Coast at the table, is a kind of magic. You made the magic.

No. 6

You Keep The Receipts.

You forwarded me a 2012 email in 2024 with no commentary. You sent me your family tree without explanation. You remember things. You know what they mean. You give them back to people. I learn from you, every time you do this.
